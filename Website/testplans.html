Straight Line Movement

Objective:

Ensure the rover maintains a consistent travel direction.

Test Procedure:

1. Fully charge the rover's battery and ensure the motor controller is functioning properly.

2. Place the rover on a level test track.

3. Run program to move the rover forward for 3 seconds.

4. Conduct five test runs, recording distace at multiple checkpoints (e.g., every 1 foot).

5. Repeat the test on different terrain types such as concrete, grass, and sand.

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The rover’s speed must remain within 2-5 inches of deviation from the straight line for at least 80% of the test duration.

2. Any deviation exceeding 5 inches from the specified reference line is a failure.

3. If the rover exceeds or falls beyond the deviation limit for more than one trial, modifications must be made to the motor control system before retesting.

Consistent RF Communication

Objective:

Verify that the rover can sustain continuous operation for a minimum of 30 minutes without a loss of power or system failure.

Test Procedure:

1. Fully charge the battery before testing and ensure all sensors and subsystems are active.

2. Operate the rover on a test course under normal working conditions, including GPS navigation, obstacle avoidance, and payload carrying.

3. Record the time until the rover stops or experiences a significant drop in performance.

4. Conduct multiple tests to determine if battery life is consistent across different conditions (e.g., smooth terrain, rough terrain, variable inclines).

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The rover must remain functional for a minimum of 30 minutes without significant power loss or performance degradation.

2. If the rover ceases to function before 30 minutes in more than one test, the power system must be revised (e.g., larger battery, optimized power consumption).

GPS Accuracy (±3 ft)

Objective:

Confirm that the rover can reach a designated GPS waypoint with an accuracy of ±3 feet.

Test Procedure:

1. Select 10 random GPS waypoints in an open field with minimal interference.

2. Command the rover to navigate autonomously to each waypoint.

3. Measure the actual stopping position relative to the target using a high-accuracy GPS device or a laser rangefinder.

4. Repeat the test on rough terrain and under different weather conditions to assess consistency.

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The rover must reach within 3 feet of the designated GPS location in at least 8 out of 10 trials.

2. If the rover deviates by more than 3 feet in more than 2 trials, recalibration of the GPS module and navigation algorithm is required.

Radio Range (50 ft)

Objective:

Verify that the rover can maintain stable communication with the RF transmitter up to a distance of 50 feet.

Test Procedure:

1. Place the RF transmitter at a fixed location in an open area with minimal signal interference.

2. Move the rover away from the transmitter in 10-ft increments while monitoring signal strength.

3. Record the distance at which communication is lost or significantly degraded.

4. Conduct the test in environments with possible interference, such as urban settings or areas with obstacles.

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The rover must maintain uninterrupted communication at 50 feet.

2. If the signal is lost before 50 feet in more than two tests, an improved antenna or signal amplifier is needed.

Payload Drop Mechanism

Objective:

Ensure the rover successfully deploys the payload at a designated location.

Test Procedure:

1. Load the rover with a 1.5” x 1.5” x 1.5” wooden block (simulated payload).

2. Command the rover to navigate to a predefined drop zone.

3. Activate the payload drop mechanism and record the landing position.

4. Repeat the process 10 times under different terrain conditions.

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The payload must land within 1 foot of the designated drop zone in at least 9 out of 10 attempts.

2. Failure to release the payload or excessive deviation from the target indicates a need for adjustment to the drop mechanism.

Obstacle Avoidance

Objective:

Ensure the rover avoids obstacles and maintains a safe clearance distance of at least 1 inch.

Test Procedure:

1. Set up a test course with static obstacles of different sizes and materials (e.g., walls, poles, rocks).

2. Command the rover to navigate through the course while logging its proximity to each obstacle.

3. Use a laser rangefinder or ultrasonic sensors to measure the closest distance the rover approaches an obstacle.

4. Introduce moving obstacles to test dynamic avoidance responses.

Pass/Fail Criteria:

1. The rover must never come within 1 inch of an obstacle in at least 9 out of 10 test runs.

2. If the rover contacts an obstacle or violates the clearance threshold, adjustments must be made to the avoidance algorithm.
