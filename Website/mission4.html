Mission 4 Report

Advanced GPS Navigation

Result: UPCOMING

The rover must successfully complete advanced navigation tasks using GPS waypoints and RF communication while maintaining robustness and environmental resilience.

Task 1: Waypoint Navigation

  • The rover must navigate to a specified GPS waypoint provided beforehand.
  • GPS waypoint coordinates may be hardcoded for this mission.
  • The rover must stop within 15 feet of the marked GPS waypoint.

Task 2: Dynamic Waypoint Navigation and Return

  • The rover must receive GPS coordinates from an RF transmitter beacon located at the starting point.
  • It must place a physical marker within 15 feet of the specified waypoint.
  • After placing the marker, the rover must return to its original starting location.
  • The distance between the physical marker and the marked waypoint, as well as the distance between the start and end positions of the rover, will be measured.
  • The cumulative error (marker placement + return accuracy) should not exceed 25 feet.

GPS Data Transmission

  • The GPS coordinates of the waypoint will be transmitted as a 12-character message.
  • The latitude coordinate consists of five digits preceded by ‘N’, while the longitude consists of five digits preceded by ‘W’.
  • The five latitude digits represent decimals appended to 39; the five longitude digits represent decimals appended to -108.
  • For example, a waypoint at 39.09495N, -108.58784W would be transmitted as: N09495W58784.
  • Coordinates will be continuously transmitted by the RF beacon at 5-second intervals.

Performance Conditions

  • Full credit requires successful mission completion on rough terrain.
  • Extra credit will be awarded for successful completion on sandy terrain.
