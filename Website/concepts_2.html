Chassis Concept Documentation

The chassis serves as the structural foundation for both Hyperion and Theia, providing support for all essential components, including mobility systems, electronics, and payloads. The chassis concept evaluation focused on effort to design, reliability, customizability, and ease of assembly to ensure optimal performance for each rover’s mission requirements.

Hyperion: Tank-Style Rover

Hyperion is engineered for extreme durability and resilience in demanding environments. Its 3D-printed frame is designed to provide structural integrity while minimizing weight, ensuring optimal performance in rugged terrain. The frame is reinforced to withstand high-impact forces and external stressors, making it ideal for traversing rocky, uneven landscapes. The modular construction allows for easy repairs and modifications, enhancing adaptability for long-term missions in unpredictable conditions.

Theia: Car-Style Rover

Concept Evaluation

Concept Evaluation Criteria

To ensure optimal drivetrain selection, the following weighted criteria were analyzed:

  • Price (12.5%): Cost-effectiveness of manufacturing or procuring components.
  • Effort (12.5%): Complexity of integrating and assembling the drivetrain.
  • Reliability (25%): Structural integrity and durability of the treads or wheels in demanding environments.
  • Customizability (50%): Ability to tailor the design for specific terrains and operational needs.

Final Decision & Implementation

The final weighting and analysis results determined that the 3D-printed frame outperformed the purchased frame overall, offering significant advantages in price, customizability, and sufficient reliability while maintaining manageable assembly effort. Given the mission needs of each rover:

  • Hyperion (Tank-Style Rover): Will utilize a 3D-printed frame to ensure a balance of durability and adaptability for challenging terrain.
  • Theia (Car-Style Rover): Will also leverage a 3D-printed frame, benefiting from its cost-effectiveness and design flexibility to meet mission requirements.

Payload Assembly

The final analysis and testing results confirmed that the selected payload assembly design for Hyperion best meets the mission requirements by balancing secure deployment, modularity, and durability. The approach ensures the payload remains stable during navigation and can be deployed accurately at designated locations.

  • Hyperion (Tank-Style Rover): Will use a servo-actuated payload release mechanism integrated into the 3D-printed chassis, ensuring precise and reliable payload drops in varied terrain conditions.
  • Theia (Car-Style Rover): Will utilize a spring-loaded payload ejection system, optimizing for quick-release functionality while maintaining structural integrity in high-speed movement scenarios.

This implementation maximizes efficiency, minimizes failure points, and provides consistent payload delivery, critical to Hyperion’s operational success.
